<p>On 3 April, the U.S. asked for Turkey's support for the Manbij offensive, but Turkey had two demands in exchange for helping the U.S.-led anti-ISIL coalition. Turkey first demanded that the Syrian Arab tribes to be included in the Manbij operation should leave the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Democratic_Forces" title="Syrian Democratic Forces">Syrian Democratic Forces</a>, which mostly consists of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Protection_Units" title="People's Protection Units">People's Protection Units</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Protection_Units" title="Women's Protection Units">Women's Protection Units</a>, and also the U.S. should increase its airstrikes for groups Turkey supports.<sup id="cite_ref-two_demands_84-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two_demands-84">[84]</a></sup> On 4 April, according to a Turkish source, a group of U.S. military and intelligence staff traveled to Turkey to work on a plan for an operation to capture Manbij.<sup id="cite_ref-two_demands_84-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two_demands-84">[84]</a></sup> Turkey stated that it would not participate nor contribute to the offensive,<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> the reason being the involvement of Kurdish (YPG) fighters and because it was beyond the range of artillery stationed in Turkey.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> Furthermore, Turkish intelligence and military units were to closely watch the operation in Manbij.<sup id="cite_ref-hurriyet_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurriyet-87">[87]</a></sup> When the offensive started, the <i><a href="/wiki/Washington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a></i> reported it under the headline of "Ignoring Turkey, U.S. backs Kurds in drive against ISIS in Syria".<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
